Galway

I loved exploring the cute industrial city of Galway, located about 3 hours away from Dublin. Galway is known for its vibrant arts scene, lively music, and charming medieval architecture. I spent my time wandering the winding streets, popping into local shops and pubs, and enjoying the lively atmosphere.

My favorite places included Eyre Square, John F. Kennedy Memorial Park, the Waterway Trails, and Galway's Cathedral. The city involved a great amount and balance of city like ambiance and natural scenery. I found the people to be incredibly friendly and welcoming, and I enjoyed the lively music scene and local pubs.

I also enjoyed visiting Galway's Computer and Communications Museum, where I learned more about the city's tech and innovation scene. The museum had fascinating exhibits on the history of computing and telecommunications in Ireland and worldwide. I also loved spending time playing with the vintage computers and video games on display, particularly PacMan.

Overall, Galway was a highlight of my study abroad journey in Ireland. The city's charming atmosphere, rich history, and welcoming locals made it a truly memorable experience. The city is full of charming pubs, lively music, and friendly locals. I had a wonderful time exploring the unique culture and history of this vibrant city.

The city's location on the coast also makes it a great base for exploring the nearby Cliffs of Moher, which we will discuss on another page here!

For more information about Galway and its attractions, you can visit their website: Visit Galway.
